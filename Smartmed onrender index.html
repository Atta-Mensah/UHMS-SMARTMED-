Sample Medical MCQ

Neurology • M2 Level

2 min

A 65-year-old man presents with progressive weakness. Which finding is most suggestive of Guillain-Barré syndrome?

A
Symmetrical ascending weakness
B
Ascending paralysis with areflexia
C
Unilateral facial droop
D
Muscle wasting and fasciculations

Explanation

Guillain-Barré syndrome typically presents with ascending paralysis and loss of reflexes. CSF shows albuminocytologic dissociation.
